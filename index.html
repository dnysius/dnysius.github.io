Tell me about yourself!

I’m a recent grad who achieved a BSc. in physics at University of Toronto, with minors in both statistics and maths. My goal is to make big ideas more accessible and human-centric through storytelling. I’ve studied countless formulas and concepts, investigated many phenonema through experimentation, and used computational techniques to analyze data, all throughout my undergraduate program.

I’m currently transitioning onto a digital transformation career path, where I can leverage my multifaceted skillset to help companies and causes not get left behind - by adapting to the rapidly developing technological landscape.

I managed to keep up with many of my talented peers in upper-level physics courses, but I really exceled in laboratory work which involved a lot of hands-on diagnoses and analyses. I frequently used Python and LaTeX throughout my studies, mostly for physics, and learned a bit of R in statistics courses. I've read many papers and did writings like this one here and this one. I'm interested in mental health and wellness; taking ideas from clinical psychology research and seeing ways they can help myself and those close to me. You can sometimes find me on stage playing guitar in various bands where I get paid with food. Speaking of food: you'll probably catch me playing with food in the kitchen or dining at some of my favourite food places around the GTA whenever I'm taking a break.

In 2020, I took two statistics courses, one on regression analysis (final report here) and one on machine learning (first project here, second project here).
